Why is dental care so important for dogs?
Dogs have an impressive set of teeth, which is equally suitable for grasping and holding as well as for tearing and cutting individual pieces of meat. For this reason, the set of teeth of the dog is vital to survival. Veterinarians point out that 80 percent of all dogs over the age of three require dental or gum treatment. The diagnosis here is tartar, gingivitis and periodontitis, which can lead to tooth loss. A scheduled dental care is the best to keep your dogâ€™s teeth healthy. It is important to start with an animal-friendly care before the dog suffers from dental problems this is where our ultrasound toothbrush steps in.

How does it work?
Dogs are very sensitive to sounds and motion. OurÂ ultrasound toothbrush works completely without vibration and beeping. The Piezo chip in the brush head generates up to 96 million oscillations per minute which break up the plague. The special ultrasonic toothpaste creates micro-bubbles, which "implode" and clean the teeth, gums, palate and tongue in the most effective way. Visiting us regularly for our Sonic teeth cleaning treatments helps to effectively prevent gingivitis, periodontitis and calculus, mouth odour and tooth loss.

ProTraining: Qualified Pet First Aider

Pets have accidents the same as humans and emergency training is essential for a dog groomer. Animal Welfare Act 2006

Here at the dog house we follow the animal welfare act 2006. We take positive steps to ensure your dogs are cared for properly and provide the five welfare needs. A suitable environment, suitable diet, need to be able to exhibit normal behaviour patterns, need to be protected from pain, suffering, injury and disease and free from fear and distress.
